3/12/19: The Origin Story

I'm pretty sure my first thoughts of living life on the road came after I did a bit of traveling in Europe after college. I got that cliche thirst for endless travel that most people do after they do a bit of backpacking and started watching a unhealthy amount of travel documentaries and youtube videos. The one thing about my europe travels is that I was mostly traveling to big cities because they were easy to get to by train or bus. I really wanted to see the less touristy country sides and wanted to be able to come and go as I pleased without having to make sure I didn't miss trains or planes. That's when I stumbled across what's known as the Pan American Highway.

For those of you who have never heard of this, it's a road, or more a conglomerate of roads, that spans from Alaska all the way to the tip of Argentina (picture below). The route is driveable completely by car except for a small section known as the Darian Gap. This area is pretty interesting and if you want to know why its impassable I suggest reading up on it here. People would travel in vans or jeeps across all of South America coming and going as they pleased. It seemed like true travel to me, where I could explore at my own pace. The idea of spending a year to travel this mind blowing 20,000 miles became somewhat of a new life goal that I could not get out of my head. I have a tendency to become hyper obsessed with things that really peak my interest and I just had a hard time letting this one go. I would tell friends and family about this dream and I would mostly get weird looks and “Ah yeah, whatever you say Eric”s back as responses but I knew that if I really wanted to do it, I could make it happen.

Now as you can see I’m not traveling South America and I don't think I will be anytime soon. I won’t say that I have given up on that dream but taken a bit of a detour while still keeping the idea within reach. It honestly would have been a lot to take on all at once. I don’t speak any latin languages fluently. While I consider myself a very big DIYer (Im a cheap bastard), I’m not the most mechanically inclined when it comes to cars. And honestly I didn't know if I would like living in a car alone, so embarking on a year journey in foreign countries may not have been the wisest decision. Also I had no where near the money I would have needed to fund such a long trip safely. So that leads us to about a year ago where the idea of a much more attainable goal of living in a Van and traveling the US came about. I could still have the freedom of living life on the road and visiting new places while keeping my mothers blood pressuruytd5 at a normal level.

I believe it is a good starting point to feel out life on the road and so far it's pretty much how I pictured it being. Things are going well and I’m enjoying living life in a small metal box so at this moment the Pan American Highway is still a goal and a dream, but no one knows what the future will bring and I’m perfectly okay with that.
